Understanding Spectral Data

A prism receives white light and separates it into all its wavelengths—the visual light spectrum. This represents spectroscopy at its most basic form—dividing light into different wavelengths.


A prism represents a simple form of spectroscopy

A more complex system used for astronomy that utilizes the same basic mechanics


Spectral data is the different levels of energy emitted by celestial objects across various wavelengths on the electromagnetic spectrum. This spectrum spans from visible light, which our eyes perceive, to X-rays, infrared, ultraviolet, and even radio waves.


A spectral emission graph of the Sun, with varying energy levels corresponding to different wavelengths.

The intensity and patterns of radiation at different wavelengths carries a wealth of information about the object such as its chemical composition, temperature, velocity, and magnetic fields. By deciphering these spectral signatures, astronomers can unveil the secrets of galaxies, stars, and cosmic phenomena.

Analyzing Spectra

Each type of atom only absorbs or emits specific wavelengths of light, making them easy to identify in a spectral graph.

... spectra of a hydrogen atom

Below: the absorption (left) and emission (right) spectra of a hydrogen atom

left: when the atom absorbs light, the energy used to absorb the light results in a dip in the graph at certain wavelengths; right: likewise, the energy from those wavelengths is emitted as radiation—these wavelengths are unique to hydrogen only!

Selected Discoveries

NASA’s Webb Reaches New Milestone in Quest for Distant Galaxies

December 9, 2022

Some of the earliest known galaxies were confirmed and analyzed using spectroscopic observations, revealing their redshift and characteristics, shedding light on their properties and the universe's early evolution.

Discovering new worlds with quantitative spectroscopy

February 28, 2023

The Vatican-Potsdam Northern Ecliptic Pole (VPNEP) survey took spectroscopic data from over 1,000 stars for potential exoplanets, yielding precise parameters for each star and paving the way for future insights into star-planet relationships.

Discovery Alert: Webb Maps and Finds Traces of Water in an Ultra-hot Gas Giant's Atmosphere

May 31, 2023

Water vapor was discovered in the atmosphere of the exoplanet WASP-18 b. They also found temperature variations and indications of a strong magnetic field affecting its winds, providing insights into how such unique planets form.

The analysis of these patterns to unravel the intricate mysteries of the universe is an art form unlike any other—and one that should be accessible by anybody.

Instruments

Spectrographs are the instruments used to measure spectroscopic data. They are contained within both telescopes and observatories. Two spectral imaging instruments were recently launched aboard the James Webb Space Telescope, NirCAM and MIRI.
